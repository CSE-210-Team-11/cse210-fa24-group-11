## **What Went Wrong**
1. **Miscommunication and context gaps**: Some tasks were initiated without complete understanding, leading to rework.  
2. **Documentation lag**: Progress and decisions were not consistently documented, making it harder to onboard members mid-sprint.  
3. **Git conflicts**: Divergent practices in resolving merge conflicts caused delays and required additional effort to standardize.  
4. **Delayed design finalization**: Design discussions stretched into the implementation phase, occasionally impacting focus and alignment.

---

## **What Went Right**
1. **Clear sprint priorities**: Defined and revisited priorities, allowing the team to stay focused on critical deliverables.  
2. **Efficient reviews**: Faster review cycles with timely approvals and merges, minimizing blockers.  
3. **Strong collaboration**: Active team collaboration through pair programming, scrums, and quick problem-solving sessions.  
4. **Improved user focus**: Regular feedback during code reviews emphasized usability and alignment with end-user needs.  

---

## **Things to Keep**
- **Sprint priority alignment**: Regularly revisiting priorities to maintain focus and agility.  
- **Thorough testing**: Ensuring all new features are tested before merging.  
- **Timely code reviews**: Providing constructive and timely feedback during pull request reviews.  
- **Team support culture**: Maintaining the collaborative and supportive atmosphere demonstrated during the sprint.  

---

## **Things to Change**
1. **Documentation improvements**:
   - Allocate specific time for documenting progress and decisions during the sprint.  
   - Begin early documentation for all new features or architectural changes.  
2. **Design process adjustments**:
   - Start each sprint with a dedicated **brainstorming and design alignment session** to clarify requirements.  
3. **Git workflow standardization**:
   - Ensure all team members are comfortable with the agreed-upon Git workflow to reduce conflicts and rebase delays.  
4. **User-centric perspective**:
   - Shift towards a more user-centric approach by emphasizing end-user impact during development discussions.  

---

## **Key Takeaways**
Sprint 1 demonstrated the team's ability to deliver high-quality work under tight deadlines. By addressing the identified challenges and leveraging the positive practices established, the team is well-positioned for even greater success in the next sprint.
